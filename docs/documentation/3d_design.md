## **Subtractive and Additive**

Additive design is the process of creating a shape by building it up with layers of material. For example, while using a 3D printer you add layers until the object is finished.

Subtractive means you create a shape by removing material from a solid block, like cutting, carving, or drilling away parts to get the final design.

## **Parametric Design**
In parametric design, a model's size and shape are controlled by numbers, so changing a number simultaneously changes the design.

## **What is pressfit?**
Pressfit is a way of joining parts so they stay together without screws or glue. The parts are made to fit tightly and hold in place by friction.

<img class="photo" src="https://drive.google.com/thumbnail?id=1IPl1c1lZAlLjt-S2XZh3MIxDvJErzqrd&sz=w400" alt="Photo" width="500">

## **What is a kerf?**
Kerf is the small amount of material that a laser removes when cutting. You need to think about it when designing parts, or they might not fit together correctly.

<img class="photo" src="https://drive.google.com/thumbnail?id=1odFwMOtRHtWQbY9x7yrbzrjyBFh8ijUF&sz=w400" alt="Photo" width="500">

Photo Credit:Google


## **STL, OBJ and GCODE**

In 3D design, files like STL and OBJ store the shape of your model, and OBJ can also keep colors. G-code tells the 3D printer how to build it layer by layer. These files help turn your digital design into a real object.
