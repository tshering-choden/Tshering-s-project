## Visit to CERN

CERN stands for the European Council for Nuclear Research (derived from its original French name, Conseil Européen pour la Recherche Nucléaire).

It is the world’s largest laboratory where scientists study the tiny "building blocks" that make up everything in the universe. To do this, they use a massive underground machine called the Large Hadron Collider, which acts like a giant racetrack for particles. By zooming these particles around the track and smashing them into each other at incredibly high speeds, scientists can see what’s inside them and learn how the universe worked right after it began.

Beyond just physics, CERN is also famous for being the place where the World Wide Web was invented to help researchers share their data more easily.
