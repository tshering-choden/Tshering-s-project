## Visit to CERN

> **Author:** Tshering Choden 

> **Tags:** `CERN`, `Learning`, `Experiences`

CERN stands for the European Council for Nuclear Research (derived from its original French name, Conseil Européen pour la Recherche Nucléaire).

It is the world’s largest laboratory where scientists study the tiny "building blocks" that make up everything in the universe. To do this, they use a massive underground machine called the Large Hadron Collider, which acts like a giant racetrack for particles. By zooming these particles around the track and smashing them into each other at incredibly high speeds, scientists can see what’s inside them and learn how the universe worked right after it began.

Beyond just physics, CERN is also famous for being the place where the World Wide Web was invented to help researchers share their data more easily.

## The Frontiers of Science

The visit to CERN, the European Council for Nuclear Research, was a distinctive and transformative experience. As a global hub for high-energy physics, it allowed the group to explore the mechanisms behind the universe’s greatest mysteries.

A definitive highlight was the opportunity to meet Sir John Ellis, who is a renowned British theoretical physicist.

## Exploring Art in Italy

The trip continued to Italy, where we saw beautiful arts. In the city of Florence(birthplace of the Renaissance,), we visited the Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore. It was so massive and beautiful that it was hard to believe someone actually built it!

We also visited the home of the famous Medici family, who were known for helping great artists and students long ago. I saw famous paintings and statues by artists like Leonardo da Vinci and Michelangelo.

 My favorite was the statue of David. It stands tall and looks very brave, representing the strength and independence of the city.
