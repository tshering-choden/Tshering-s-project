## Introduction to Laser Cutting

Laser cutting uses a powerful light beam known as a laser to cut or form materials. It can create precise, tiny shapes that are difficult to cut by hand. Laser cutting is used to create jewelry, models, and signs in factories, workshops, and schools. It is accurate and faster than using normal cutting tools.

## How Laser Cutting Works in 5 simple explanation

•A powerful light beam is used by a laser cutter to carve or cut objects.

•On a computer, you make a design, and the machine follows it.

•Along the design lines, the material is burned, or melted by the laser.

•Thinner materials require less power and cut more quickly, while thicker materials require more power and cut more slowly.

•Laser cutting is very quick, accurate, and capable of creating intricate designs that are challenging to cut by hand.

## Preparing Designs for Cutting

>I opened Inkscape and sketched the design's shapes.

>To enable the Fusion Edge to accurately follow the routes, vector lines were used.

>I then sent the file to the Fusion Edge laser cutter through the PC.

>Depending on the material, adjust the Fusion Edge's focus, power, and speed.

>After that, we watched as the Fusion Edge precisely sliced the design after turning on the machine.

## Safety and Maintenance

•Safety

Keep the area ventilated and clean.

Never leave the machine unattended.

•Maintenance

Clean the machine and lenses regularly.

Check that the laser is aligned and working.

Remove dust and leftover material after use.
