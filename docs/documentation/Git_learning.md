## What is GitHub?

GitLab is a digital workspace that stores your code, tracks every change you make so you never lose your work, and uses "robots" to automatically move your website from your computer to the live internet.It’s a one-stop shop where you can write your code, track your to-do list, and launch your site all in one place.

## Installation(Process of setting up your account)

Sign Up: Create a free account at https://github.com/
New Project: Click "New project" and choose "Create blank project" to make a folder for your work.
Add a README: Check the box to "Include a README file"—this is your project's front page.
Write Content: Click "Edit" on your README and type your notes using Markdown (simple symbols for bold or lists).
Save (Commit): Click "Commit changes" at the bottom to save your progress and create a history of your work.
Use the Web IDE: For managing multiple pages or folders, click the "Web IDE" button to use a simple built-in code editor.

## Markdown

Markdown is a simple language that uses basic symbols to format your text. In GitLab, it is the main tool used to create professional README files and project notes. By using this language, you can easily add things like bold text, lists, and headings to your documents. GitLab automatically turns those simple symbols into a clean, easy-to-read layout, making your project look professional and organized.

## Repositories(Repo)

A repository is a specialized digital folder that acts as a "living history" of your project by saving everything every time you make a change. Unlike a normal folder, it allows you to track your progress, undo mistakes by reverting to older versions, and collaborate with others without overwriting their work. While platforms like GitLab host these repositories online, the repository itself is the core engine that keeps your files organized, safe, and synchronized across a team.

## Commit and push

|  Term   | Meaning
|----------|-------------
| Commit.  | * A local "save" that records your changes.  Includes a short note describing what you did.
| Push     | * An "upload" that sends your saves to GitLab.  Make your work visible to your team online.


## Pipeline errors

A pipeline error is like a "failed inspection" that happens when GitLab’s automated system finds a mistake in your code while trying to build or test it. These errors usually occur because of simple typos, missing files, or incorrect settings in your configuration. 
To solve them, you simply click on the red "failed" icon in GitLab to read the error message, which tells you exactly what went wrong. Once you fix the mistake on your computer, you just commit and push again to restart the inspection and get back on track.

## Mkdocs

In GitLab, MkDocs works like an automatic website maker. When you save your notes (Markdown files) and push them to GitLab, a "pipeline" starts working in the background. This pipeline uses MkDocs to grab your files and turn them into a real, clickable website hosted on GitLab Pages. Instead of clicking through a list of messy files, one can just visit a web link to read your organized and pretty documentation.
